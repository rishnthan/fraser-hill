Attractions

Allan's Water

Allan's Water is a tiny lake in an alcove that was converted into a park for recreational sports and paddle boating from a reservoir that had fallen into neglect over time. FH Allan, assistant to chief infrastructure engineer FW Mager, was the architect of this project, which was originally a water collecting and supply during the colonial hill station era.

Strawberry Garden

A flower nursery within the compound also provides some amusement and retail opportunities for ornamental plant and gardening enthusiasts. Finally, there's a cafe that serves local cuisine and snacks, including scones, cakes and ice cream, along with fresh strawberries and derivative products like jam. Open daily from 8am till 6pm, entrance is free to public.

Royal Fraser's Hill Golf Club

The Royal Fraser's Hill Golf Club is one of the oldest courses in the country, situated right in the center of this highland resort destination at town. This 9-hole walking course may be small, but it offers several challenging sections along with beautiful surrounding forest scenery. Fraser's Hill Development Corporation operates the public course.

Bird Interpretive Center

Dedicated to bird watching, one of Fraser's Hill's most popular tourist and visitor activities, over 250 species of birds have been recorded here, including hornbills, doves, pheasants, eagles and other endemic species. During certain seasons, migratory birds from Russia, Japan and China visit Fraser's Hill to escape freezing winters and find pasture.

Scott's Pub & Restaurant

Scott's Pub and Restaurant, formerly the Tavern, is a continental restaurant that sits on a small embankment overlooking the clock tower and town roundabout, perched next to Puncak Inn Hotel. Housed in a colonial bungalow of mock Tudor architecture, the restaurant also serves a selection of local dishes alongside its English menu, with some pastas, finger food and imported beers thrown in.

Nature Trails

Pine Tree Hill

Pine Tree Hill is a 1,500 meter high mountain that sits in Bukit Fraser. The trail head can be accessed near the end of the High Pines road, just before the bungalow after Seri Intan. The trail is about 5.5 km long, and it remains the most challenging and toughest trekking path in Fraser's Hill. The name is inspired by the coniferous plants that used to dot the peak.

Abu Suradi

Abu Suradi Trail is a short and easy 500 meter jungle walk suitable for hikers of all ages. It was named after the first person to be awarded a mining lease for Pamah Lebar - now the popular Fraser's Hill public golf course in the center of town. Abu Suradi operated a tin mine for ten years from 1899 until his lease got cancelled by British district officer.

Kindersley Trail

The trail takes its name after RCM Kindersley, who was part of the original Fraser's Hill Development Committee responsible for developing the highland retreat during colonial times. The trail was first opened in 1919 as an access route for British officials to visit the Methodist Mission from nearby homes.

Hemmant Trail

Hemmant Trail, named after Frank Hemmant, who designed Fraser's Hill Golf Course, begins from a grass clearing, just opposite of the Abu Suradi trail head. It's an easy 1km trail with a moderate ascent for the first quarter and level for the rest.

Bishop Trail

The trail is named after Reverend CJ Ferguson-Davie, who rediscovered Fraser's Hill in 1917 while looking for the original founder Louis James Fraser who had gone missing circa 1915. Ferguson and a fellow bishop failed to find any trace of Fraser, but they did recommend the area for development as a hill resort.

Mager Trail

Mager Trail is a fairly easy 350 meter hike, named after the British engineer who was responsible for the initial development of Fraser's Hill in 1918 as a colonial rest station. FW Mager erved the colonial government from 1918, helping to survey land and identify suitable places for building bungalows and asphalt roads.
